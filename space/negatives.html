The negative impacts of space exploration on the climate

According to a NOAA research article from the Journal of Geophysical Research Atmospheres, A ten-fold increase in rocket launches fueled by hydrocarbon fuels (which is very possible in the next two decades based on recent trends) would cause damage to the ozone layer and alter circulation patterns in the atmosphere. Rocket engines which burn kerosene emit exhaust that contains black carbon (A.K.A. soot) directly into the stratosphere and damages the ozone layer which protects us from the suns ultraviolet radiation. This emission of black carbon higher in the atmosphere stays for up to four years and gets warmed by the sun which in turn further warms the climate.
